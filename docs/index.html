Music Projects

Baptism

This was my final project for one of my music technology class, it is made for 8 channels surround sound. The piece is named Baptism since a friend said the middle section reminded him of the montage from The Godfather. It starts out with some interesting sounds altered using filters, frequency shift etc. with a foreshadowing bass line to the middle section. Then we advance to the more dissonant section which is finally resolved by all the channels coming together using an arrangement of MIDI instruments playing a little original melodic composition. The ending inserts some new material while being a reminiscent of the beginning.

Guusto

I spent 8 month as a full-stack junior developer co-op at Guusto, during that time I received a lot of hands-on experience developing new features, debugging and working with a team through the development cycle. I worked with HTML, CSS and AngularJs for frontend and Java with spring framework in the backend. Here are some examples of the issues I worked on:

The ability to split a received gift card to be redeemed at different partnered venues, e.g. a 100 dollar gift card can be split to be redeemed for 50 dollar at Best Buy and 50 dollar at Amazon.

An admin overview panel for managers to view the balance, money spent on purchasing gifts etc. for the different teams in their company.

Implementing a pre-existing feature in the web platform for android, the teams functionality. It allows team members in a company to nominate each other for their work, and keeps feeds + leaderboard for nomination activity as part of the employee recognition aspect of the application.

Some other notable projects that I worked on include writing a customized link shortener so we do not have to use google's link API and be bounded by their request limits (turns out they were cancelling their service also after I finished the project). Another interesting one is setting up weekly auto reminder email using sendgrid API, there are many more but instead of listing them I will put etc.
